# High-Content Analysis Assays

## Purpose

This module presents cell imaging assays used in High-Content Analysis (HCA), with a focus on methods capable of quantitatively describing phenotypic changes induced by biological, chemical, or environmental perturbations.

Throughout the module, we will study Cell Painting and Live Cell Painting as complementary strategies for phenotypic profiling. The goal is not only to learn the staining and image acquisition protocols, but also to understand how the experimental question, plate design, controls, image quality, and analysis workflow determine the biological interpretation of the results.

## Learning outcomes

By the end of this module, the learner will be able to:

- explain the principle of image-based phenotypic profiling;
- describe the evolution, applications, and limitations of Cell Painting;
- recognize the main steps of the experimental and computational workflow of a Cell Painting assay;
- identify controls, quality criteria, and common sources of artifacts;
- understand the rationale of Live Cell Painting and how it differs from classical Cell Painting;
- select a profiling strategy that is compatible with the experimental question.
