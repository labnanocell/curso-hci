# Microscopy and Image Acquisition

### Purpose

This module introduces the fundamentals of optical microscopy and fluorescence required to understand how biological images are generated, acquired, and interpreted in High-Content Analysis (HCA) experiments. Throughout the module, we will cover the physical principles of image formation, the phenomenon of fluorescence, and the main optical components involved in microscopic image acquisition.

## **Learning outcomes**

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

- understand how microscopic images are formed and why they are not direct copies of the specimen;
- explain the fundamental principles of fluorescence and their application in biological experiments;
- interpret the main parameters that influence image quality, including resolution, contrast, magnification, and detection sensitivity;
- understand how fluorophores, optical filters, and detectors affect image acquisition and data interpretation;
- evaluate experimental limitations related to spectral overlap, fluorescent panel design, and multichannel image acquisition;
- recognize image formation as an integral part of experimental design in High-Content Analysis (HCA).
