# Cell Health: Concepts, Methods, and Metrics

### Purpose

This module introduces the fundamental concepts of cell health assessment in in vitro experiments, with an emphasis on the biological interpretation of the primary endpoints used in High-Content Analysis (HCA). Throughout the module, we will discuss the concepts of viability, cytotoxicity, and cytostasis, the different categories of assays used to measure them, the main quantitative metrics derived from these assays, and best practices for analyzing, presenting, and interpreting dose–response curves.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

- distinguish viability, cytotoxicity, and cytostasis as distinct biological phenomena;
- understand what the major cell health assays actually measure and recognize their limitations;
- select appropriate orthogonal assays to strengthen the biological interpretation of experimental results;
- correctly interpret quantitative metrics such as percent viability, EC50, IC50, and other parameters derived from dose–response curves;
- recognize experimental limitations related to dose–response curve coverage, data quality, and technical interferences;
- present and interpret cell health results rigorously and in accordance with best practices in biomedical research.
